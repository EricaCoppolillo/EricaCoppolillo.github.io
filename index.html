Postdoctoral Researcher · ICAR-CNR, Italy

Erica Coppolillo

Algorithms decide what you see, who you hear, and what you come to believe. But at what cost?

To address this question, my research explores the intersection of AI and social networks, studying how algorithms shape opinions, drive engagement, and influence the flow of information in online communities. Starting from Recommender Systems, I am now focused on Large Language Models (LLMs), addressing their intrinsic biases across different perspectives.

I recently earned my PhD from the University of Calabria, Italy, under the supervision of Professors Francesco Calimeri, Simona Perri, and Giuseppe Manco. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at ICAR-CNR, as a member of the BMSA group.

Ultimately, I aim to make AI systems more transparent, accountable, and aligned with human well-being, helping ensure that the technologies we interact with every day serve people rather than silently shape them.

Research Interests

At the edge of AI and society

My research sits at the intersection of AI and its societal implications. I investigate algorithmic bias, opinion formation and diffusion, and the dynamics of complex social networks.

I began my PhD studying Recommender Systems and their associated noxious phenomena, such as popularity bias and information homogenization. Then, I have shifted focus to LLMs and autonomous agents.

My current work examines the intrinsic biases of these systems and their capacity to reshape social communities by steering human engagement. Alongside this, I am interested in grounding LLMs within structured knowledge and symbolic reasoning frameworks, such as knowledge graphs and logic programming, to advance the development of knowledge-aware AI systems.
